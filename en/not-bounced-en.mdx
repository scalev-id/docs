---
title: "How to Remove Bounced Status from Customer List"
description: "When you run an email broadcast campaign to a customized recipient list, some emails may end up with Bounced status."
---
When you run an email broadcast campaign to a customized recipient list, some emails may end up with **Bounced** status.

**Bounced** means the recipient couldn't receive the broadcast email you sent in a campaign.

Causes vary:

- Recipient inbox full

- Email server down

- Custom domain expired

- ...etc

This means that customer contact will stay Bounced until you manually remove it to **Not bounced**.

Here's how:

- Open the Customer page in the Mailev menu

<figure>

![Restore bounced](https://tutorial.scalev.id/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/restore-bounced-1-1024x616.png)

<figcaption>

Type bounce in the search box

</figcaption>

</figure>

- Find the Customer contact marked Bounced (red label)

- Click the Bounced button — this popup appears:

<figure>

![Restore bounced](https://tutorial.scalev.id/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/restore-bounced-2-1024x700.png)

<figcaption>

Click Restore

</figcaption>

</figure>

After clicking Restore, the customer's status changes to not bounced (no red label).

<figure>

![Restore bounced](https://tutorial.scalev.id/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/restore-bounced-3-1024x640.png)

<figcaption>

No longer Bounced

</figcaption>

</figure>

Once the customer contact is no longer Bounced, you can target that recipient again in the next email broadcast campaign.

Hopefully the recipient can receive your broadcast email without issues.

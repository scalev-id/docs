Gross Revenue = The final invoice amount paid by the customer

Net Revenue = Product price minus product discount minus unique code

Adjusted Gross = Product price minus product discount minus unique code minus shipping discount

Example case:

Agus checks out and buys a physical product — 1 plain t-shirt at Rp100,000 with a Rp15,000 discount and Rp25,000 shipping. Agus gets a Rp5,000 shipping discount, then chooses a Virtual Account (VA) for payment with a 5% VA fee.

So:

Product price Rp100,000  
Product discount -Rp15,000  
Unique code -Rp321  
Shipping discount -Rp5,000  
Shipping Rp25,000

Subtotal Rp104,679  
VA transaction fee (5%) Rp5,233

Total Rp109,912

From Agus's transaction, the purchase event values are:

## Gross Revenue

Rp100,000 - Rp15,000 - Rp321 - Rp5,000 + Rp25,000 + Rp5,233

So:

**Gross Revenue = Rp109,912** ← The purchase event value sent to Meta.

## Net Revenue

Rp100,000 - Rp15,000 - Rp321

So:

**Net Revenue = Rp84,679** ← The purchase event value sent to Meta.

## Adjusted Gross Revenue

Rp100,000 - Rp15,000 - Rp321 - Rp5,000

So:

**Adjusted Gross Revenue = Rp79,679** ← The purchase event value sent to Meta.

Another example:

Surti from Aceh checks out and buys 2 units of a skincare product at Rp150,000 each. Surti gets a free shipping promo for all of Indonesia.

Lucy from Jayapura also checks out and buys the same skincare product as Surti, but buys 3 units. Lucy also gets the free shipping promo for all of Indonesia.

The packages are shipped from Bandung via Ninja Express to each buyer's address.

Surti and Lucy choose Virtual Account as their payment method with a 5% fee per transaction.

So:

**Surti's order**

Product price Rp150,000 (×2) Rp300,000  
Product discount Rp0  
Unique code -Rp150  
Shipping discount -Rp113,000  
Shipping per package Rp56,500 (×2)

Subtotal Rp186,850  
VA transaction fee (5%) Rp9,342

Total Rp196,192

Surti's order purchase event values:

Gross Revenue: Rp300,000 - Rp0 - Rp150 - Rp113,000 - Rp0 - Rp9,342 = **Rp177,508**  
Net Revenue: Rp300,000 - Rp0 - Rp150 = **Rp299,850**  
Adjusted Gross: Rp300,000 - Rp0 - Rp150 - Rp113,000 = **Rp186,850**

**Lucy's order**

Product price Rp150,000 (×3) Rp450,000  
Product discount Rp0  
Unique code -Rp550  
Shipping discount -Rp313,500  
Shipping per package Rp104,500 (×3)

Subtotal Rp135,950  
VA transaction fee (5%) Rp6,797

Total Rp142,747

Lucy's order purchase event values:

Gross Revenue: Rp450,000 - Rp0 - Rp550 - Rp313,500 - Rp0 - Rp6,797 = **Rp135,950**  
Net Revenue: Rp450,000 - Rp0 - Rp550 = **Rp449,450**  
Adjusted Gross: Rp450,000 - Rp0 - Rp550 - Rp313,500 = **Rp135,950**

The **bold** numbers above are the purchase event values that will be sent from Scalev to Meta.

That's the difference between Gross Revenue, Net Revenue, and Adjusted Revenue found in the [Trigger Event Purchase](https://tutorial.scalev.id/triggerpurchase/) settings in Scalev.

You can choose whichever of the 3 purchase event value options best fits your business.
